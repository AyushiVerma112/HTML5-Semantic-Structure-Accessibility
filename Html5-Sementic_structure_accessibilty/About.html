Hi, I'm Aanya — I build the part of the web that's easy to overlook.

I'm a frontend developer based in Agra, India, who spends most of my time thinking about markup before mockups. A page that reads well to a screen reader and ranks well in search isn't an accident — it's a structure decision made early, not patched in later.

My approach

Most of my projects start the same way: a content outline turned into a heading hierarchy, before a single color or font is chosen. From there, semantic elements, ARIA roles, and labelled form controls aren't an afterthought — they're the skeleton everything else hangs on. The result is usually a site that performs well in Lighthouse audits not because I chased a score, but because the markup was honest about what each piece of content actually is.
